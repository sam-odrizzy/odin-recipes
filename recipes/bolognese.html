Bolognese: Roman Nectar

Bolognese has, for centuries, brought joy to the world. However, in 2023, Sam O'Driscoll split the history of bologonese in two (before Sam and after Sam) when he invented Sam's Spag Bol, a revolutionary take on a traditional dish. For the first time in history, he's putting fingers to keyboard and recording this dish for the world to see.

Ingredients

  • 3lbs of ground 90% lean ground beef
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 4 large carrots
  • 3 zucchini
  • 1 basket of white mushrooms
  • 2 large cans of whole peeled tomatoes
  • 1 small can of diced tomatoes

Steps

  1. Chop onions and garlic finely
  2. Put onions and garlic in large pot with olive oil. Put on low heat. You'll let these bad boys cook until translucent at low heat.
  3. Add pepper, some salt, and red peper flakes to the meat as you let it sit outsid of the fridge
  4. While the onions and garlic are cooking, get choppin! Wash the veggies
    1. Peel or grate the zucchini and carrots
    2. Chop the mushrooms
  5. Once the onions are translucent, add the ground beef and turn the heat up high
  6. Sitr vigorously ask the meat cooks. You want to make sure the meat is completely cooked, so keep stirling and pushing until it's all brown and none of the meat is red
  7. Once all the meat is cooked, add in the cans of tomatoes and stir vigorously
  8. Once the pot is boiling, turn the stove to a simmer and add in the vegetables
  9. Stir vigorously. Periodically adjust the heat as you feel. Remember cooking is an art! Let the bolognese tell you what it needs.
